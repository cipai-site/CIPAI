About CID-AI

Building decision intelligence systems with transparent reasoning and preserved human authority

Vision

Most AI systems optimize for convenience and speed, treating transparency as optional. CID-AI starts from the opposite premise: decision support that cannot be explained cannot be trusted.

We're building decision intelligence infrastructure for situations where understanding the reasoning matters as much as getting an answer. This means investors evaluating deals, advisors supporting clients, executives making strategic calls, and anyone making high-stakes decisions with incomplete information.

Core Principles

1. Transparency Over Convenience

Every recommendation includes reasoning chains, confidence scores, and explicit assumptions. Users can validate logic and identify blind spots.

2. Human Authority Preserved

AI recommends, human decides. Clear separation with full audit trail of who decided what and why.

3. Uncertainty Acknowledged

Systems should be honest about confidence levels. Better to flag uncertainty than overstate certainty.

4. Longitudinal Context

Decision quality improves when systems understand objectives, constraints, and patterns over time through Personal Decision Models (PDM).

5. Architecture for Evolution

Early-stage implementation with production-grade architecture. Built to evolve into persistent decision intelligence infrastructure.

Technical Approach

Modular Analysis

Domain-specific modules (CFO, CLO, etc.) provide specialized analysis with explicit reasoning chains. Each module operates independently but feeds into unified decision context.

Personal Decision Model (PDM)

Confidence Scoring

Every claim, inference, and recommendation includes explicit confidence rating. System distinguishes between high-confidence assertions backed by data and lower-confidence extrapolations.

Audit Trail

Complete record of reasoning steps, data sources, assumptions made, alternatives considered, and final decision with rationale. Built for governance, compliance, and retrospective analysis.
